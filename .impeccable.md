## Design Context

### Users
Early-stage tech founders (pre-seed to Series B) in crypto, AI, B2B SaaS, and embedded finance. Evaluating Bastille as an embedded operator — not a passive advisor. Decision-making context: they've been referred or found the site organically, they're sizing up credibility and fit before booking an office hours session. Desktop-primary, professional setting.

### Brand Personality
Sharp, insider, credible. An operator who knows the room and doesn't perform for outsiders. The Bastille/1789 reference is intentional — countercultural, historically grounded, slightly dangerous. Not a brand that explains itself to people who don't get it.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme. Editorial/poster energy with operator precision. The riso-print hero, the "1789" ghost watermark, the tricolor (cobalt/pink/lime echoing the French Tricolore) — these are the right instincts. The grid-separator approach (1px rules creating structure) is correct. Bebas Neue for display is correct.

**Anti-references:** Generic VC/advisory sites (white, clean, safe), agency portfolio vibes (overstyled, full-page animations), generic SaaS landing pages (pricing tiers, hero metrics, Inter, rounded corners).

### Design Principles
1. **Insider, not introductory.** The site should read like something written for people who already understand the landscape. No hand-holding.
2. **Operator precision over consultant polish.** Structured, systematic, direct. Tables and lists over cards and icons.
3. **The tricolor is a system.** Cobalt = method/growth, lime = output/CTA, pink = brand/raise. Use it consistently or abandon it.
4. **Typography carries the brand.** Display type (Bebas Neue) is doing the heavy lifting — body type needs to match its intelligence, not undercut it.
5. **One voice, not five fonts.** The current five-family stack (Bebas Neue + DM Serif Display + DM Sans + Outfit + Playfair Display) is incoherent. Reduce to two, maybe three with a clear role hierarchy.
