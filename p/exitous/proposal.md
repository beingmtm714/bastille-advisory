## What I Heard

Our conversation surfaced three structural problems.

**First**, the buyer timing is broken: HR teams managing a RIF are budget-constrained, distracted, and often facing their own exits.

**Second**, the event driving product usage — a layoff — is episodic, which creates a ceiling on any recurring subscription model.

**Third**, there are five parallel channel bets (HR direct, outplacement partners, benefits brokers, consultants, federal grants) with no sequencing logic between them.

The founding story is real and rare. Keita built this after witnessing the human cost of the Google and Meta layoffs firsthand. That matters — but it hasn't been translated into the commercial pitch the right buyers need to hear.

---

## What I Found

The dedicated offboarding software market sits at $244M today.¹ The broader outplacement services market is $5B+, growing at 7%+ annually — driven by structural AI-led workforce restructuring, not cyclical downturns.² The established players (Lee Hecht Harrison, Right Management, RiseSmart) own enterprise.³ Nobody owns the SME segment or the post-employment experience layer. Rippling, BambooHR, and Workday handle IT offboarding; none of them have built what Exitous is building — personalized, expert-backed guidance for the employee after they walk out the door.⁴

SMEs are the fastest-growing segment in outplacement at 10%+ CAGR.⁵ Benefits brokers and PEOs already serve this market and are actively looking for differentiated products. That's the wedge.
