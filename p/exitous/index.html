Exitous:
Perspective & Potential Support

For Keita Wangari & Stacy

What I Heard

Our conversation surfaced three structural problems.

First, the buyer timing is broken: HR teams managing a RIF are budget-constrained, distracted, and often facing their own exits.

Second, the event driving product usage — a layoff — is episodic, which creates a ceiling on any recurring subscription model.

Third, there are five parallel channel bets (HR direct, outplacement partners, benefits brokers, consultants, federal grants) with no sequencing logic between them.

The founding story is real and rare. Keita built this after witnessing the human cost of the Google and Meta layoffs firsthand. That matters — but it hasn't been translated into the commercial pitch the right buyers need to hear.

What I Found

  • The dedicated offboarding software market is $244M today. The broader outplacement services market is $5B+, growing at 7%+ annually — structural AI-led workforce restructuring, not a cyclical blip.
  • Enterprise is owned. Lee Hecht Harrison, Right Management, and RiseSmart have locked it up. Nobody owns the SME segment.
  • The post-employment experience layer is wide open. Rippling, BambooHR, and Workday handle IT offboarding. None of them have built personalized, expert-backed guidance for the employee after they walk out the door.
  • SMEs are the fastest-growing segment in outplacement at 10%+ CAGR. Benefits brokers and PEOs already sell into this market and are actively looking for differentiated products to bundle.
  • PEOs are a strong GTM. They sit between Exitous and hundreds of SME clients, own the HR relationship, and have direct incentive to reduce offboarding liability for their book of business.
  • Recruiting firms are the other lever. Laid-off, actively-searching talent is pre-qualified pipeline firms will pay to access. The right entry point is firms like Riviera Partners — but the volume play is second and third-tier firms who don't have proprietary sourcing advantages and need the pipeline more.
