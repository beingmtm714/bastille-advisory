Background
↓ expand

The problems Bastille works on are consistent across industries: founders building something new, trying to close the gap between what they've built and what the world is ready to see. The methodology transfers because the gap is universal.

At SignalFire, an AI-native venture firm built around proprietary data infrastructure, I worked most closely with the AI engineers and founders before the current era had a name — developing the fund-side perspective on what narrative actually moves capital when the technology is genuinely new.

At XPRIZE, running frontier technology competitions meant positioning breakthrough ideas to audiences who had never encountered them. That's the same problem every early-stage founder faces, in every vertical.

At Ava Labs, that methodology became Codebase, the flagship Avalanche accelerator. Founders placed on VC watchlists, institutional partnerships formed, raises closed — the playbook ran across a diverse cohort of companies.

The work is designed to be embedded, not advisory. I'm in the room when it matters, not reviewing a deck after the fact.
