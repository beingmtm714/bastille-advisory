How it works

EMBEDDED, NOT ADVISORY

Most advisory relationships produce decks. This one produces work. The distinction matters at the stage where the list of things that need to happen is always longer than the time to do them.

The model is simple: you should be in the rooms and relationships where your presence is irreplaceable — your highest-value customers, the investors who need to hear it from you, the partners where trust is the product. Everything that can be systematized, delegated, and run in parallel doesn't need to be in your calendar.

On GTM, that means you're building relationships with your most important users while I'm building the systematic approach for tier two — gathering their feedback, finding signal in the noise, and feeding it back in a form that informs the next move. On narrative, you're making the pitch while I'm stress-testing it across audiences you haven't been in front of yet. On a raise, you're in the conversations while I'm building the pipeline, prepping the data room, and making the introductions that create options.

The point of embedded engagement is that it compounds. Every week of structured execution makes the founder's time more leveraged, not less.
