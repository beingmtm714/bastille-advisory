## Executive Summary

Brickroad's product is real and ahead of its narrative, with eight of the top ten target funds already in trial. Three structural problems currently limit the business: trial conversion cycles run closer to six months than six weeks, sales and operations responsibilities sit entirely with the founder, and the investor and buyer narrative remains underbuilt ahead of a September raise on a three-month clock.

Diagnostic work from an initial engagement identified four gaps that resolve into a single commercial function: sequencing which buyer vertical follows hedge funds, a single narrative spine serving both buyer and investor audiences, a closing motion that differs by buyer type, and retention ownership with no current owner.

This proposal scopes the two workstreams that require attention before September. Raise readiness leads, given its shorter timeline. Commercial architecture follows, scoped and ready to build.

## The Build

**Three-month engagement · $250/hr.** Two workstreams, sequenced, with the option to extend as needs evolve.

**Raise Readiness** — *Leads · on-ramp week of July 27*
Preparation for the transition off founder-led sales beginning August 1.

- Refinement of the existing narrative into a single deck, with figures kept current across all investor communications
- Pitch coaching through recurring working sessions, with the deck and narrative sequencing pressure-tested against real feedback
- Investor-aligned milestones defined using existing relationships, reflecting what investors actually fund rather than assumed benchmarks
  - Usage proxies defined to carry the narrative ahead of revenue: daily searches, dataset pulls, and platform use for ideation, framed as a clean climb toward the J-curve
  - Advance exposure to investors ahead of the raise opening, to build signal in advance
- Investor relations preparation and warm introductions, including pipeline development, list-triage tooling, and follow-through to close

**Commercial Architecture** — *Follows · build-ready end of August*
The retention plan designed ahead of the raise, rather than improvised during it.

- Evaluation of the current retention system and definition of success metrics, internal and client-facing, for the current and following quarters
- Backwards design from identified gaps, incorporating enterprise best practices
- Client-facing retention northstars
- Tactical processes and playbooks for deployment as the client base scales

## How It Would Work

- **Requirements:** confirmed monthly hours to finalize the retainer fee, and resolution of two open scoping questions — whether all trials are currently free, and whether smaller, buyable products exist that could compress the six-month cycle.
