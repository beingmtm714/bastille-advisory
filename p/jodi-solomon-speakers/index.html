For Katie Dupuree

Where things stand

Neil carries 90–95% of the revenue, and he winds down after 2027. You want ten names each holding a slice of that instead of one name holding all of it. Better spread, less exposure.

The venue side is already yours. Paramount, the Wilbur, the Tobin, the Roberts Group, the promoters who spider-web a name out across the country. When you have the talent, booking them is a phone call.

The work runs two pipelines at once, built as one system:

  • Buyer reactivation. Your existing roster and venue relationships, worked again. This one starts first and brings revenue soonest.
  • Talent acquisition. I research a wide pool of unrepresented talent who can sell tickets, score them, and hand you a ranked shortlist to pursue. Ten new names is the target.

I build that system as one phase. Two more grow it once it's running, one on each pipeline.

The build

Phase 1 · The System $7,500

Everything it takes to stand up the outbound machine and hand it to you, built in roughly six weeks across six workstreams.

  • Foundation$1,000

    I turn your ACT export into two clean lists, talent and buyers, with contact, last engagement, last paid, and status. I dedupe them and append what's public: current site, recent press, active tours. Records untouched for years won't have clean history to recover, so I flag what's usable.

  • Scoring$900

    I work with you to define the criteria, then rank both lists. Talent by what drives ticket sales: social following, podcast reach, book momentum, recent tours, audience age, and whether someone already represents them. Buyers by money tier and recency.

  • Outbound$1,900

    I build the outreach and hand you the keys, every credential yours. Warmed email sequences for buyers under your own domain. Personal drafted DMs for the younger talent who don't live on email. Warm relationships as a note from you, not a template.

  • Triggers and triage$1,400

    A speaker's book or press moment queues a drafted outreach to the venues that fit, ready for you to approve and send. I set the triage rules for replies, bounces, and follow-ups.

  • Playbook$700

    The whole system documented so you run it without me: sequences, triage rules, message library, and how to work a name from research to booked.

  • Website$1,600

    A roster-forward redesign that reads current to the talent you want to sign, plus two intake forms: buyers requesting a speaker, talent asking about representation. One feeds your buyer list, the other feeds your sourcing.

What's not included

  • Running the outbound and triage day to day once it's live. I can operate it on a separate monthly retainer, or it stays with you and the playbook.
  • Tool subscriptions (email sending platform, enrichment, social) and any paid media. You hold those accounts and cover the cost. I'll recommend the stack and a rough monthly.
  • Talent contract negotiation and booking. That stays yours, and it's what you're good at.
  • A full brand identity rebuild. The website is a roster-forward refresh. Broader brand work is its own scope.

Beyond the build

Two phases, one on each pipeline. Both are short lifts, because the machine from Phase 1 already does the heavy part. Once it's running, we move into these.

  • Phase 2Widen the buyer base

    The same outbound machine, pointed at new demand.

    • Corporate and conferences. High flat fees, no ticket risk, booked year-round, which breaks the spring-and-fall trap Neil created. Some of these contacts already sit in your CRM. New segment, same system.
    • Season packages. Sell P1 venues a season instead of a single date, three or four speakers across the year at a bundled rate. Lecture series is already a format you run. Recurring and predictable, and it turns three sells into one.
    • College routing. The CRM clusters campuses by geography, so a speaker headed to one school picks up the others within a short drive on the same trip. More dates per travel day, an easier yes.
  • Phase 3Turn venues into scouts

    Your venues see who sells tickets in their market before anyone else, and right now that signal dies at their box office. I set up a path for a room to flag an unrepresented name, you sign and tour that person, and the venue that found them earns a finder's fee or a small override, or gets first date when the act comes through. It runs on the Phase 1 CRM and trigger: a sourced name is a new link between your two lists, and the system already knows how to push a hot name back out to the network. Your highest-signal talent scouts are buyers you already have.
