Keyflex AI:
Perspective & Potential Support

For Camille Yan & JR Pagdanganan

Overview

Keyflex AI is an operating system for midterm rental operators, built by people who ran the workflow themselves. Incorporated April 2026. First agent in production by May. Coming out of MTR Summit: one paying customer at $1,000/month, one stalled LOI, 76 warm leads, and 306 attendees in the database. The product is stable enough to promise to new customers. The open questions are what to charge, who to close first, and how to build a fundraising pipeline that reflects what's been proven.

Three areas are running on instinct where they should be running on process: pricing and cohort strategy, operator pipeline activation, and fundraise operations. The decisions in each area cascade into the others. Pricing determines who to go after. Who you go after determines what JR builds. What JR builds determines what you can show investors. Month 1 builds the foundation across all three at the same time, because they can't be sequenced.

The Engagement

Three months, defined in advance, evaluated at the end of each. Month 1 is 30 hours and produces six deliverables — playbooks and frameworks Camille and JR own and run after I'm out of them. Months 2 and 3 are each 25 hours: running the sprint cadence with JR, refining pricing against real market data, following through on investor conversations, and bringing in introductions from my network in vertical AI. Month 3 closes with a process handoff and a cohort read-out.

Month 1 — 30 hours / $5,250
  • Pricing & Cohort Strategy~6 hrs
    The three customer tranches you described — self-serve, core SaaS at 20–40 units, custom/enterprise at 100+ — want different things from the product and will pay different amounts for it. This work maps each tranche to what can ship today versus in 30 and 90 days, then builds the pricing playbook: structured tests at increasing price points, feedback capture, tier-by-tier framing, and criteria for when to move the number up or hold. Revenue forecast built at different close rates.
  • Design-Partner Conversion~4 hrs
    Camille is running tier-one conversations. This work is about making those conversations land: what to ask, how to frame the design-partner ask, how to handle objections by type, when to close versus keep warm. The output is a playbook she can run without me in the room — plus the materials that go with it: a one-pager and a follow-up template with send timing. Target: 3–5 confirmations from the MTR Summit lead list.
  • Operator Engagement~7 hrs
    76 warm leads and 300 attendees is a segmentation problem before it's an outreach problem. This work tranches the list, builds the interview script, runs stakeholder interviews directly with tier-two and tier-three operators, stands up the private update channel, and defines the follow-up cadence and criteria for graduating contacts from interview to active conversation. The output is a playbook Camille owns — not a one-time list pull.
  • Product Prioritization~3 hrs
    JR is making build decisions against competing inputs from design partners. This work, built with JR, is the mechanism that translates cohort feedback into sprint inputs — so the roadmap moves on customer evidence rather than what's technically possible that week.
  • Fundraise Ops~10 hrs
    Start with who Camille and JR already know: operators, angels, advisors, anyone with a check to write or a name that opens rooms. Build the pipeline doc, stage every contact, define the outreach sequence and meeting prep framework. Build the investor and advisor deck. Prep Camille and JR for every conversation. By month end: all first-round angel and strategic conversations done or actively scheduled, with a clear read on who's moving and who isn't.
Month 1 Deliverables
  • Customer tranche map with feature availability by tier (today / 30 days / 90 days)
  • Pricing Playbook: price discovery test sequence, feedback capture mechanism, tier-by-tier framing guidance, criteria for when to move the number up or hold, revenue forecast at different close rates
  • Design-Partner Sales Playbook: question set for tier-one conversations, objection handling by type, close vs. hold criteria, one-pager for prospects, follow-up template and send timing
  • Operator Engagement Playbook: segmentation criteria for the full lead list (76 warm leads and 300 attendees tiered), stakeholder interview script, completed interviews with tier-two and tier-three operators, private update channel stood up and seeded, follow-up cadence and content rhythm, criteria for graduating contacts from interview to active conversation
  • Product prioritization framework: cohort feedback → sprint input process, built with JR
  • Fundraise Operations Playbook: contact mapping process and completed map of everyone Camille and JR already know, pipeline staging criteria, outreach sequencing, meeting prep framework, investor and advisor deck working draft, first-round angel and strategic outreach done or actively scheduled with a status read on each
Month 2 — ~25 hours / est. $4,375
  • Feedback Synthesis & Sprint Inputs~6 hrs
    Camille is running daily calls with design partners. This work takes that raw input, structures it into a ranked feature queue for JR, and filters out the outlier requests that would pull the roadmap off course. The output drops directly into JR's sprint. Where the product prioritization framework from month 1 defined the process, this is the first full execution cycle — in it with JR, not handing it off.
  • Sprint Planning with JR~4 hrs
    Two two-week sprints scoped for the month, built from the cohort synthesis. The work here is translation: customer signal into sprint inputs, and a pressure-test on whether what JR plans to ship matches what the cohort asked for. By the end of month 2 this cadence should be something JR can run without me — month 2 is where we find out if the framework holds under real sprint pressure.
  • Pricing Playbook Iteration~5 hrs
    Month 1 pricing tests have results. This work analyzes what came back, produces a working recommendation on the number to pitch and how to frame it by tier, and updates the revenue forecast at different close rates. The Pricing Playbook gets its first real revision — adjusted for what the market actually said, not what we modeled.
  • Fundraise Follow-Through~7 hrs
    Month 1 conversations are resolved or in motion. Anyone who said yes or maybe gets a clear next step. Anyone who went quiet gets one more touch. Managing the pipeline alongside Camille — not reporting on it from the outside. The pipeline doc stays current. No false optimism.
  • Operator Engagement Iteration~3 hrs
    Review what the month 1 interviews produced, adjust the script where the signal says to, and flag which interview completers are warm enough for Camille to move into a direct conversation. The Operator Engagement Playbook gets its first real test — month 2 is where we find out what the cadence actually does to contact temperature.
Month 3 — ~25 hours / est. $4,375
  • Investor Narrative~7 hrs
    Retention data, a tested price point, design partners on record. The deck shifts from "here's what we're building" to "here's what we've proven." This work sharpens it into something you'd put in front of a fund — and updates the Fundraise Operations Playbook to reflect what the first two months of conversations actually taught us about who moves and who doesn't.
  • Investor Intros & Prep~8 hrs
    Month 3 is where my network comes in — warm intros to pre-seed funds and angels in vertical AI. Every intro carries context. Prep before every meeting, debrief after. Not hand-offs: in the room on the prep and on the read-out.
  • ICP & Cohort Read-Out~5 hrs
    Cross-cohort analysis of everything the design partners and stakeholder interviews produced across two months: does the 20–40 unit ICP hold, does pricing converge, what does the signal say about the next cohort? A short memo that either confirms the strategy or names where it needs to adjust. This is the first real test of whether the playbooks produced durable signal or noise.
  • Process Handoff~5 hrs
    The four playbooks built in month 1 and iterated in month 2 get their final version here — documented so Camille owns all of them going into month 4, regardless of whether the engagement continues. The handoff includes a short walkthrough session with Camille so nothing lives only in my head.
