# Speaker Acquisition System & Website Redesign

Bastille Advisory · July 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Jodi Solomon Speakers Bureau is seeking to diversify its revenue base by expanding its roster with new speaking talent capable of driving ticket sales.

The objective of this engagement is to build a systematic capability for identifying, evaluating, and onboarding new talent, reducing reliance on any single source of revenue.

This proposal establishes a system for candidate research, evaluation, and outreach, supported by a redesigned website aligned to the audience this new talent represents. The venue relationships required to book new talent are already in place; the gap this engagement closes is identifying and securing the talent itself.

## The build

**Phase 1 · The system · $7,500**
Delivered in approximately six weeks.

**Ideal Candidate Profile**
Collaborative discovery process to define what makes a candidate worth pursuing.

- One structured discovery session to define candidate criteria
- Written candidate profile document, including criteria and weighting
- Client sign-off required prior to sourcing

**Sourcing**
Research and compilation of a candidate list against the approved profile.

- List of approximately 1,000 unrepresented speakers matching the candidate profile
- Per-candidate research: recent publications, press coverage, social growth, podcast activity, tour history
- Organized candidate list delivered for scoring

**Scoring**
Ranking of the sourced list against the candidate profile.

- All sourced candidates (~1,000) ranked against the candidate profile
- Shortlist of the top 10 priority candidates
- Documented rationale for each ranking

**Outreach System**
Selection and configuration of outreach tooling for the ranked candidate list.

- Evaluation and selection of outreach tool(s) matched to candidate channels (email, LinkedIn, social)
- Full configuration: sequencing logic, follow-up timing, deliverability safeguards
- Review-and-send workflow for all outreach, requiring one action per message
- Documented process for reuse in future campaigns

**Signal Monitoring and Triage**
No existing tool performs this function. A custom monitoring system is built and delivered as software you own outright.

- Custom-built software, owned entirely by Jodi Solomon Speakers, not a subscription or licensed product
- Monitors the sourced candidate list for public signals: book deals, press coverage, and social activity
- Automated signal classification and drafted outreach generated for each qualifying signal
- Operates on approximately $25–$50 per month in data costs (or $225–$350 with expanded social monitoring), billed directly to client accounts

**Website**
Redesign of the existing site to appeal to the new, younger market segment this system targets, since the current site was not built with that audience in mind.

- Competitive research on comparable speaker bureau and talent websites
- One design consultation session to align on direction
- Full website redesign, roster-forward and contemporary in tone and design
- Integration with existing tools, preserving current buyer and talent intake functionality

**Playbook**
Full documentation of the system to support independent operation.

- Written documentation of the candidate profile, sourcing methodology, scoring rubric, outreach configuration, and triage rules
- Handoff package enabling operation without ongoing engagement

## Beyond the build

Once this system is running and you want to widen it:

**Corporate and conferences.** High flat fees, no ticket risk, booked year-round.

**Season packages.** Sell P1 venues a season instead of a single date. You already run this format for lecture series; this extends it.

**College routing.** Cluster campuses by geography so a speaker headed to one school picks up others on the same trip.

**Venues as scouts.** Your venues see who sells tickets in their market before you do. A path for a room to flag an unrepresented name, you sign and tour them, and the venue earns a finder's fee or first date when the act comes through.
