Confetti
2025
Worked with Confetti's founder to design and execute a campaign that became the platform's highest-revenue engagement since its founding — $1.5M in transaction volume across 32 teams in 30 days.
GrowthNetwork Leverage

Confetti had a strong product — an onchain incentive platform operating across 20+ blockchain ecosystems — but needed a flagship campaign to demonstrate its ceiling. The founder needed to prove the platform could move serious volume, generate meaningful adoption with a top-tier ecosystem partner, and establish a repeatable campaign format. The question was what kind of engagement would actually drive that.

The engagement: bring Confetti into Avalanche as the infrastructure for a 30-day onchain bracket tournament — 32 ecosystem teams competing in daily head-to-head rounds on Confetti, community members voting with AVAX tokens, voters whose picks advanced receiving a share of the prize pool. The mechanic aligned adoption with outcome: projects competed for mindshare, voters competed for returns, and every round drove a new transaction cycle through Confetti's platform. The campaign design was built to maximize Confetti's transaction volume while giving Avalanche a credible, high-engagement community activation.

The campaign generated $1.5M in transaction volume over 30 days — the highest-revenue engagement in Confetti's history. For the founder, it established Confetti as the go-to infrastructure for ecosystem-scale incentive campaigns, created a replicable playbook for future chain partnerships, and delivered a reference case that any ecosystem partner could immediately understand.

Approach

Bastille's model is built on a simple observation: the founders who raise successfully are the ones who knew what investors needed to see — and built toward it before the process opened. Most advisory enters at the deck. By then, the gaps are already visible. Bastille enters earlier — and does the work alongside you through close. The three phases below map the full arc. Most engagements focus on one or two — scoped to where the leverage is. This playbook wasn't built advising startups. It was built embedded with AI-native founders at SignalFire before the wave hit, building BD and narrative systems across a fund portfolio at M13, and running protocol-level ecosystem growth at Avalanche. That's the lens individual companies get access to.

01
Weeks 1–2
Define Milestones
Investor Expectations Mapping
What does "good enough to raise" look like in your specific category right now? Most founders answer this wrong — because they're defining the bar themselves instead of going to the people who set it. We source the answer directly: VC conversations across your category, combined with industry comps and current deal data, to map the specific metrics — growth, retention, CAC, TVL — that investors are actually underwriting at your stage. You leave with a clear, defensible milestone set and an honest read on where you stand against it.
Round Targeting
Round size, structure, and the investor profile that makes sense for your stage and category. Built around what you can actually get to, not what looks good on paper. You leave with a grounded, specific fundraise target.
02
Weeks 3–5
Close the Gaps
GTM & Product Strategy Execution
This isn't advice you implement on your own. Bastille works alongside the team — refining ICP, sequencing outreach, pressure-testing positioning, designing and running the experiments that close the gap between where you are and where investors need to see you. Warm intros to vetted dev, design, and tokenomics partners when needed. You leave with a GTM motion actively running and a metric story that holds up in diligence.
Narrative & Materials
Deck and data room built around the metrics — not the other way around. Narrative rebuilt from first principles when needed: what the company is, why now, what traction means in this context. VC-insider input on what investors are actually pattern-matching for. You leave with materials that are ready to go out.
03
Weeks 6–8
Run the Process
Investor Map & Warm Intros
A targeted list of the right investors for your category, stage, and round size — not a spray. Warm introductions into top-tier VCs and angels across AI, crypto, and traditional venture. Intros go out when the business is ready, not before. The network is the starting point. The preparation converts it.
Process Management
Structured conversations designed to build conviction, not just friendly interest. Feedback loops that tell you where you actually stand. Process managed through close — so momentum doesn't die between meetings. You leave knowing exactly where each investor is and why.
On engagements: The phases above map the full arc — but engagements don't have to. Some founders come in at 01 and run through close. Others need one piece: GTM execution, narrative, investor outreach, or process management. Work is scoped to where you are. Typically 6–8 weeks. Bastille takes a limited number of clients at any given time.
About
The Firm

Bastille was built on a simple observation: the most valuable things an accelerator offers — a sharp network, focused narrative, someone who actually does the work — don't require a cohort model or an equity check. They require experience, relationships, and judgment.

The firm works with founders in AI, crypto, and infra — categories where the distance between investor interest and a closed round comes down to knowing exactly what the bar is right now. That bar isn't guessed at. Every engagement starts by going to the source: VC conversations across your category to map the metrics investors are actually underwriting today — growth rates, retention thresholds, CAC, TVL. The framework that guides all execution is built from those conversations.

What distinguishes Bastille from most advisory is execution. Founders aren't paying for advice they implement on their own or introductions that sit in an inbox. The work is hands-on: GTM and product strategy refined and run alongside the team, narrative built around proven metrics, investor process managed through close.

The name comes from Bastille Day — July 14, 1789: urgency, conviction, and the decision to move when the window is open.
