What I Found

  • The digital product passport market reaches $1.78B by 2030 at 45.7% CAGR; blockchain supply chain hits $33.25B by 2033. Companies closing contracts now build the reference relationships that define the category.
  • GS1 — 2 million user companies, 10 billion daily transactions — is structurally a non-profit. It cannot own the commercial layer. Blockchain authentication is 54.3% of the authentication and brand protection market, but the for-profit, standards-writing, multi-vertical contract holder doesn't exist.
  • Blockticity is the only company positioned to write the ASTM standard and hold live contracts across critical supply chain verticals simultaneously. GS1 can't compete commercially. IBM's platform failed at execution. The position is unoccupied.
  • Demand is structural, not cyclical. The US is import-dependent on 12 critical minerals entirely and 29 more partially. Section 232 investigations now cover polysilicon, semiconductors, pharmaceuticals, and copper. Authenticated provenance is a compliance requirement, not a product feature.
  • ASTM committee participation is the primary GTM channel. 150+ companies entered CIRPASS in 2024 solely to influence DPP standards — whoever writes the standard gets the first call. F49.07 replicates that dynamic, and Blockticity holds the chair.
  • Walk Global is the volume play and the warning. Walmart reduced traceability from 7 days to 2.2 seconds, then shelved the platform — a commercial failure, not a technical one. ASTM mandates compliance rather than requesting it; layer-zero sits beneath existing systems; Section 232 answers the ROI question before the conversation starts.
